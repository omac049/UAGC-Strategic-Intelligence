📚 Graduate Student Borrowing: What's Changing

The biggest change to graduate student aid in decades - effective July 1, 2026

💡 Bottom Line for Graduate Students

If you're planning graduate school, you'll have strict borrowing limits instead of unlimited federal loans. Plan accordingly!

Before July 2026
Current System
Grad PLUS Loans

✅ Unlimited borrowing up to cost of attendance

✅ Available to all graduate students

✅ No aggregate cap

Example: MBA Student

Could borrow $80,000/year for 2-year program = $160,000 total

After July 2026
New System
No More Grad PLUS

❌ $100,000 lifetime cap for graduate programs

❌ $200,000 lifetime cap for professional programs

❌ $20,500/year maximum for most programs

Same MBA Student

Can only borrow $20,500/year = $41,000 total

$119,000 funding gap!

Changes to Pell Grant application and FAFSA timeline

Pell Grant "Stacking" Changes
Old Rule:

Pell applied first; other aid reduced accordingly

New Rule:

Pell applies last; if other gift aid covers COA, Pell is withheld

FAFSA Timing Changes
2024-25 cycle: Delayed to December 31, 2023
2025-26 cycle:
  • Pilot release: October 1, 2024 (limited)
  • Full release: December 1, 2024
Future goal: Restore October 1 opening permanently

Negative Effects

Part 4: Military & Veteran Student Aid Changes

Impact analysis for active duty, veterans, and military families

🇺🇸 Bottom Line for Military & Veterans

The 90/10 Rule remains unchanged - protecting current military student strategy. However, new accountability metrics and loan changes require attention.

Wins for Military & Veteran Operations
90/10 Rule Remains Intact
  • No change to federal revenue ratio calculation
  • Military Tuition Assistance (TA) and GI Bill® benefits continue to count toward the 10% non-federal revenue requirement
  • → Protects current military student strategy
Gainful Employment Rule Excluded
  • Senate version omits Biden-era gainful employment updates
  • Avoids Title IV risk exposure for certificate and career programs
  • → No immediate reporting burdens added
Regulatory Rollbacks & Delays
  • Borrower Defense and Closed School Discharge rules delayed until 2035
  • → Reduces regulatory complexity
Caution Areas
New "Do No Harm" Earnings Floor
  • Programs can lose Title IV eligibility if graduates earn below baseline income for 2 of 3 years
  • Compared to high school or bachelor's grads in-state
  • → Evaluate military-preferred programs (Criminal Justice, Homeland Security) for compliance risk
Grad PLUS Loan Eliminated
  • Existing borrowers grandfathered for up to 3 years
  • → Could affect veteran or spouse graduate enrollment if GI Bill is exhausted
Parent PLUS Loan Capped
  • $20K/year; $65K lifetime cap
  • → May impact dependents attending UAGC
